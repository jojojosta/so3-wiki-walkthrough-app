2. Game Mechanics

i - Battle System

The battle system in Star Ocean 3 is very unique. Battles occur in real time, not turn based so there's always something going on. In order to initiate a battle either:

  • It occurs automatically during a scene
  • You come into contact with an enemy on a map

When in battle you can do numerous things. You can attack with X (Weak Attack) and Circle (Strong Attack), which will cause you to automatically run up and engage the enemy you are targeting. You can also press Triangle to bring up the menu where you can cast symbology (magic), use items, change equipment, change your tactics, use tactical skills (like Charge), or run away.

You can also cycle quickly between your active party members (up to three) with L1 and R1.

ii - Strong/Weak Attacks

As mentioned before you can attack with both the X and Circle buttons.

  • X (Weak Attack): Initiates a quick attack which can be blocked by the enemy's guard. Consumes less Fury.
  • O (Strong Attack): Initiates a slower attack that *cannot* be blocked by the enemy's guard and will break their guard status if they are blocking. Consumes more Fury.

iii - Short/Long Range Attacks

Another difference between attacks is the range. At the top left of the battle screen it will tell you if you're in Short (S) or Long (L) range mode, which is determined by how close you are to your target enemy. Depending on the range you will perform different attacks/skills. There are four basic attack inputs:

  • X button (Short range)
  • X button (Long range)
  • O button (Short range)
  • O button (Long range)

Battle Skills are also assigned to these four slots (hold vs. tap).

iv - Fury Gauge

The Fury gauge is both a limiter and a helper. Everything you do in battle consumes Fury, including running and attacking. When your Fury reaches 0%, you cannot attack and must wait briefly for it to recharge (usually about 1 second).

Enemies also have a Fury meter (visible on difficulties below 4D). When your Fury meter is at 100%, you gain a 'Protect' status, making you immune to weak attacks and preventing flinching. Fury is also responsible for the 'Guts' mechanic, potentially keeping you alive at 1 HP when an attack would otherwise defeat you.

v - Battle Skills

Up to four battle skills can be assigned per character, corresponding to the four attack inputs:

  • X button (Short range)
  • X button (Long range)
  • O button (Short range)
  • O button (Long range)

Skills are activated by holding the corresponding button instead of tapping it. Each Battle Skill costs Capacity Points (CP) to equip. Your maximum CP increases as you level up, capping at 15. You cannot equip skills if their total CP cost exceeds your maximum.

Characters also have Support Skills which can be equipped in the skill menu. These also cost CP but provide passive effects that are always active.

vi - Status Skills

There are four status skills that can be improved using Skill Points (SP) earned upon leveling up:

  • HP (Hit Points)
  • MP (Mental Points)
  • Attack (ATK)
  • Defense (DEF)

Distributing SP allows you to customize character growth in these areas.

vii - Anti Attack Auras (Tactical Skills)

Anti-Attack Auras (AAA) are the visual effect surrounding your character when Fury is at 100% (Protect status). The default AAA stuns enemies who hit you with a weak attack while it's active.

You can find Textbooks throughout the game that unlock new AAA effects (like dealing damage, healing, etc.). These are equipped via the Tactical Skills menu and are automatically used when Protect activates.

viii - Cancel Bonus

Cancel Bonus increases the damage of Battle Skills when you chain them together. You activate it by using a Battle Skill immediately after a normal attack connects, or immediately after another Battle Skill connects.

The damage multiplier increases with each successful cancel in a chain:

  • Normal Attack -> Skill: 175%
  • Skill 1 -> Skill 2: 200%
  • Skill 2 -> Skill 3: 250%
  • Skill 3 -> Skill 4 onwards: 300% (Caps)

This is a key mechanic for maximizing damage output.

ix - Symbology

Symbology is Star Ocean's term for magic. Spells are cast in real time from the battle menu (Triangle) and resolve while combat continues.

Some characters who are primarily fighters may also learn spells. To use these spells in battle, they must equip the "Common Attack Symbols" or "Common Support Symbols" support skills.

x - Bonus Battles

The Bonus Battle gauge fills as you fight. When it reaches maximum, the next attack that connects triggers a bonus effect for that battle, consuming the gauge.

The bonus depends on the type of attack that fills the gauge:

  • Strong Attack (O): Double Fol
  • Weak Attack (X): Triple Exp
  • Symbology: Increased chance for item drop
  • Battle Skill: Increased HP/MP recovery after battle

Every five battles, another bonus orb is added randomly to the pool. The chain breaks and the gauge resets if your controlled character takes a critical hit, is incapacitated (0 HP or 0 MP), or if you escape from battle.

xi - Ring of Disintegration

Obtained in the Urssa Lava Caves near the end of Disc 1. Press Square to toggle it on/off. When active, approach breakable objects (walls, debris) - a green wireframe appears if it can be destroyed. Press Circle to fire the ring.

This is required to access hidden areas, treasure chests, and achieve 100% map completion in many areas.

xii - Private Actions

Private Actions (PAs) return, but work differently. Instead of manually triggering PA mode, your party automatically splits up when entering most towns.

You can then find and interact with your party members (and sometimes other NPCs) around the town. Conversations often involve dialogue choices.

These choices affect hidden relationship values between characters, influencing the ending you receive. PAs marked with PA in this guide are optional events affecting relationships. Some plot-related events function similarly but are mandatory.

4. Starting a New Game

When starting a new game, you'll configure these settings:

Display Setting
4:3 (Standard Screen): For normal TVs.
16:9 (Wide Screen): For widescreen TVs. Switch TV to full mode.
Audio Setting
Monaural: For mono TVs.
Stereo: For stereo TVs/speakers.
Dolby Pro Logic II: For 5-channel surround sound systems with a DPLII decoder.
Headphones: Optimized for standard headphones (use DPLII for Dolby headphones).
Event Setting
Enable Event Skip: Allows skipping scenes (including first-time ones). Use cautiously!
Disable Event Skip: Prevents skipping cutscenes.
Voice Setting
Subtitles and Voice: Full audio and text, lines can be skipped manually.
Voice Only: Audio only for voiced lines.
Subtitles Only: Text only for voiced lines (excludes some movies).
Automatic Subtitles and Voice: Full audio and text, advances automatically (cannot skip lines manually).
Vibration Setting
ON: Enables controller vibration.
OFF: Disables controller vibration.
Difficulty Setting
Earth Level: Easiest setting. Battle Trophies cannot be collected.
Galaxy Level: Normal difficulty. Standard experience, Battle Trophies can be collected.
Universe Level: Hard difficulty. Must be unlocked.
4D (Fourth Dimension) Level: Very Hard difficulty. Must be unlocked after Universe.

Note: Ensure you have at least 1200 KB free on your memory card if you intend to save Battle Trophy data.

Exp: 50,000 | Fol: 110,000 | Drop: N/A

Battle Trophies: Defeat Dark Eye within 1 min, Defeat Dark Eye without taking damage.

Strategy:

Very annoying boss due to high HP/MP and its tendency to fly away, making it invulnerable. Dodge the red circle attack easily. Main challenge is the length of the fight.

Defeat within 1 minute:

Extremely difficult. Use ranged attackers (Maria) when it flies. Build cancel bonus to 300% while it's ascending. Hope for good RNG. Petrify resistance is helpful.

Defeat without taking damage:

Takes even longer. Tip: Don't heal your party's HP/MP fully; its draining attack can't drain what isn't there. Requires lots of revival items. Stay distant.

  • Continue up to the save point and save.
  • Enter North room, examine orb -> Scene. Receive Sacred Orb.
  • Return to Aquios.
  • Enter throne room -> Event.
  • Prepare to travel to Mosel (via Peterny -> Surferio -> Passage -> Dunes). Save in Surferio.
  • Adray permanently joins now if you took him temporarily and haven't filled 8 slots.
  • Head through Passage from Parch to Plenty -> Scene & Boss fight on Sanmite Steppes.

Legion Dragon: HP: 3000 | MP: 192 | Lvl: 17 | Weak: N/A | Half: N/A | Immune: Wind | Drain: N/A | Exp: 80 | Fol: 8 | Drop: Crafting Materials (3%)

Strategy:

Very easy fight. Dragons are weak. Defeat them first, then focus on Schweimer. He poses little threat compared to recent bosses.

  • Continue Northwest to Surferio, then West through Passage to Mosel Dunes.

Boss: Chimera

Name: Chimera | HP: 200,000 | MP: 28,000 | Lvl: 75 | Weak: N/A | Half: Fire, Wind | Immune: N/A | Drain: N/A

Exp: 17,000 | Fol: 5000 | Drop: War God's Symbol (100%), Cooking Ingredients (3%)

Battle Trophies: Defeat Chimera within 1 min, Defeat Chimera without taking damage.

Strategy:

Not overly difficult. Stay behind it to avoid fire breath, but watch out for the paralyzing tail. Juggle it with combos. Equip paralysis protection if needed.

Defeat within 1 minute:

Possible. Be mindful of its high Fury; use circle attacks/skills to break guard. Keep offense high.

Defeat without taking damage:

Party might struggle alone. Support with healing items and maybe paralysis protection gear on them.

  • Run North through Mosel Dunes (Aqua Veil removes water limit). Save at the entrance to the ruins.
  • Enter Ancient Ruins of Mosel.

Boss: Stone Golem

Name: Stone Golem | HP: 300,000 | MP: 20,000 | Lvl: 85 | Weak: N/A | Half: N/A | Immune: Earth, Water, Wind, Fire | Drain: N/A

Exp: 60,000 | Fol: 71,000 | Drop: Synthesis Materials (100%)

Battle Trophies: Defeat Stone Golem within 1 min, Defeat Stone Golem without taking damage.

Strategy:

Slow attacks, not very strong. Good opportunity to practice cancel combos. Heal when needed.

Defeat within 1 minute:

High HP, but doable with strong offense. Use circle attacks to break guard if its Fury rises.

Defeat without taking damage:

Patience is key. Stay back, let party fight, heal them. Use Symbology to speed it up.

  • (Optional) Complete the Ancient Ruins of Mosel Underground dungeon now (see Optional Dungeons section).
  • Go down the West stairs -> Event, then another event.
  • Exit to Firewall.
  • **Last Chance:** Exit Firewall immediately to reappear outside. Collect two chests (Synthesis Materials, Perfect Berries). Do final shopping, Item Creation (Orichalcum weapons recommended).
  • Re-enter Firewall when ready to proceed to the final areas.

xix - Firewall

Map Completion: 1/60 Scale Bunny | Items: N/A | Enemies: Attack Bat, Elder Eye, Lv4 Robo Gunner, Masque, Stalker

No treasure. Follow directions carefully to avoid polyhedrons (obstacles).

  • Section 1 Navigation:
    • Go West.
    • Continue forward.
    • Take first left (West).
    • Continue North.
    • Continue through passageway exit.
    • Take North exit to next major area.
  • Section 2 Navigation (Polyhedrons & Switches):
    • Cannot go through dark blue doors. Head East into lower door.
    • Go up to top of room, exit West.
    • Go North to top of next room, head West.
    • Go North. At fork, enter door on right.
    • Follow to end, head South, then East.
    • Make way to Northwest corner, exit North.
    • Head to Northeast corner, exit East.
    • Activate switch in center. Leave room (West).
    • West exit is now available, go that way.
    • Go West, then down. Follow path West through door (closes behind).
    • Save game.
    • Go back East (to door that closed), head North.
    • Ignore first East branch. Ignore second South branch. Reach fork.
    • Go North. Next fork, North again.
    • Follow path to very end, enter door leading North.
    • Exit North again, then East.
    • Go to East side -> Activate second switch.
    • Return all the way back to the save point.
    • Door beside save point is now open. Save, then enter -> Scene & Boss.
Boss: Battlecopter

Name: Battlecopter | HP: 200,000 | MP: 30,000 | Lvl: 85 | Weak: Water | Half: Fire | Immune: N/A | Drain: N/A

Exp: 20,000 | Fol: 53,000 | Drop: Machinery Materials (3%)

Battle Trophies: Defeat Battlecopter within 1 min, Defeat Battlecopter without taking damage.

Strategy:

Relatively low HP for this stage. Can rise up like Dark Eye but less often. Stay behind it to avoid rapid fire. Back off when it rises to avoid fire rain. Hit hard and fast.

Defeat within 1 minute:

Low HP makes this achievable. Use moments it rises to build cancel bonus.

Defeat without taking damage:

Use ranged attacks (Deep Freeze if available). Heal party with Magical Berries if needed (can reload save after trophy if you want to conserve).

  • Save game after battle.
  • Enter teleporter -> Event.

    • Return to broken elevator room. Head Northeast into door.
